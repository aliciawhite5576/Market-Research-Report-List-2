<p><p>The automotive starting battery market consists of two types: maintenance-free batteries and conventional batteries. Maintenance-free batteries are designed to require minimal or no maintenance throughout their lifespan. These batteries are sealed, eliminating the need for adding water or checking electrolyte levels regularly. On the other hand, conventional batteries require periodic maintenance, including checking and topping up electrolyte levels. While maintenance-free batteries offer convenience and ease of use, conventional batteries are cheaper and can be easily repaired. Both types cater to different customer preferences and budgets in the automotive starting battery market.</p></p>
