<p><p>Differential pressure sensors for HVAC (heating, ventilation, and air conditioning) applications are devices used to measure the difference in pressure between two points within an HVAC system. They play a crucial role in maintaining the efficiency and performance of HVAC systems by monitoring and controlling air or fluid flow.</p><p>These sensors are primarily used in HVAC systems to measure air velocities, filter monitoring, flow monitoring, and as feedback for variable air volume (VAV) systems. They help to ensure the proper functioning of the HVAC system by providing accurate readings and enabling adjustments to airflow rates and pressure differentials.</p><p>The market for differential pressure sensors in HVAC applications is expected to grow significantly during the forecast period. <p><p>Differential pressure sensors are commonly used in HVAC systems to measure the pressure difference between two points. There are two main types of these sensors available in the market: </p><p>1. Digital Differential Pressure Sensors: These sensors provide digital output signals that can be easily read and processed by electronic devices. They offer high accuracy and reliability, making them suitable for modern HVAC control systems.</p><p>2. Analog Differential Pressure Sensors: Unlike digital sensors, analog sensors provide continuous output signals that represent the pressure difference. They are commonly used in older HVAC systems and offer lower accuracy compared to digital sensors but are still reliable for basic pressure monitoring.</p></p>
