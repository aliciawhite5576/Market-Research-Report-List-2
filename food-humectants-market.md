<p><ul><li>Natural</li><li>Synthetic</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Food humectants are substances added to food products to prevent moisture loss and maintain their desired texture and freshness. There are two types of food humectants: natural and synthetic. Natural humectants are derived from natural sources like fruits and vegetables, such as glycerol and honey. They are often preferred by consumers seeking natural and organic products. On the other hand, synthetic humectants, like propylene glycol or sorbitol, are chemically produced additives. They are widely used in the food industry due to their cost-effectiveness and ability to extend product shelf life.</p></p>
