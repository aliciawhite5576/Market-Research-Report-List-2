<p><p>Low Dropout (LDO) linear regulators are widely used in various applications to provide stable and regulated voltage output. These regulators can be categorized into two types based on the transistor technology used, namely, PMOS and NMOS. PMOS-based LDOs are suitable for low-power applications due to their low quiescent current, enabling improved efficiency. NMOS-based LDOs, on the other hand, are preferred for high-power applications as they offer better load regulation and higher current capability. Choosing the right market type depends on the specific power requirements and application needs.</p></p>
