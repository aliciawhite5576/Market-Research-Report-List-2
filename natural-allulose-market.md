<p><p>Natural allulose is a low-calorie sweetener that occurs naturally in small amounts in certain fruits like figs and raisins. It is a monosaccharide sugar that has a similar taste and texture to regular sugar but with significantly fewer calories. The growing health consciousness among consumers, coupled with the increasing prevalence of diabetes and obesity worldwide, has resulted in a surge in demand for healthier alternatives to sugar. Natural allulose's unique properties make it an attractive option for food and beverage manufacturers looking to develop low-sugar or sugar-free products without compromising taste and texture.</p><p>Moreover, the expanding food industry and the rising trend of clean label ingredients are expected to contribute to the market's growth.
