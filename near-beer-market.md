<p><p>Near beer refers to a type of beer with a low alcohol content, typically less than 0.5% by volume. The market for such beverages can be categorized into two types: limit fermentation and dealcoholization method. In the limit fermentation method, the brewing process is halted before the alcohol content reaches the standard level, resulting in a naturally low-alcohol beer. On the other hand, the dealcoholization method involves removing alcohol from regular beer through various techniques, such as vacuum distillation or reverse osmosis, while preserving the flavor and character of the original beer.</p></p>
