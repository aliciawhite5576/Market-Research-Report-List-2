<p><p>Plating on Plastics (POP) refers to the process of applying a metal coating onto plastic surfaces. There are different types of POP markets: chrome-based, nickel-based, and other metal-based. Chrome-based POP involves the deposition of a thin layer of chromium onto the plastic, providing enhanced aesthetics and corrosion resistance. Nickel-based POP is similar, but with a layer of nickel instead, offering good adhesion and electrical conductivity. Other metal-based POP includes coatings of metals like gold, silver, or copper, depending on the desired appearance or functionality of the plastic surface.</p></p>
