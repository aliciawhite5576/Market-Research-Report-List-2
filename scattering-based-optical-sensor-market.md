<p><p>The scattering-based optical sensor market includes three types: Raman, Rayleigh, and Brillouin. Raman scattering involves the emission of light due to the inelastic scattering of photons. It is used for various applications including temperature and pressure sensing, as well as in healthcare for disease detection. Rayleigh scattering involves the elastic scattering of photons and is commonly used for distributed fiber sensing, such as seismic monitoring and oil pipeline leakage detection. Brillouin scattering is also used in fiber optic sensing, particularly for temperature and strain measurements in telecommunications and structural health monitoring.</p></p>
